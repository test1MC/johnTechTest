Balance Waterfall

The Balance Waterfall module identifies the balance flows that contributed to balance or account change between two periods.

Dimension Migration Details

 

In certain situations, especially when filters are active, there may be changes in a set of portfolio balances that occur outside of the standard balance flows. This is caused by non "switch" driven re-mapping or migration of various account or customer attributes. A common example is geographic re-mapping. If a customer was in the "New York" market, but has moved to the "New Jersey" market - their balances would "disappear" from New York but not due to any traditional balance flow. This discrepancy would be captured in the Dimension Migration flow.  

Dimension Migrations can be triggered by any account dimension that is not related to product "switch" - when a filter is active for that dimension and value.  For example, if an account moves between markets A and B across two time periods, but the market filter is set to All - the dimension migration will have no impact on the balance waterfall. However, if the filter is changed to select only market B, the balances will be missing from the 'start' summary (when assigned to market A), and the dimension migration will have contributed to the overall balance change between start and end balances.

For any discrepancy over $10, details of the exact cells that make up the balance discrepancies captured in the Dimension Migration flow can only be viewed in a drill-down when a single, consecutive time period is being analyzed (i.e., a selected month for the Monthly Dataset and a week for the Week Over Week Dataset). These can be set using the Start Date and End Date drop-downs.

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

This screen is intended to answer strategic questions like:

  • What balance flows had the largest or smallest impact on portfolio changes across a given time period?
  • Why is there often a marginal discrepancy of balance details between the end-of-month balance on the Bank Data Total Balances module and the balances displayed on the Bank Data Balance Flows module?

 

How do I use this Module?

 

Use the Balance WaterFall module to understand how your institution's balance flows are impacting product portfolio changes over time.

1.    Access the Bank Data section (choose Balance Waterfall), and use the multi-select Data Filters on the left side to narrow down your institution's unique portfolio segmentation (i.e., Product Group, Term Bucket, etc.).

The default starting selections are All for all Segmentation Filters.

2.    Choose the Dataset (Monthly or Week over Week) and enter the Start Date and End Date you want to test.

Note: You must click to apply changes.

3.    Select a metric and relevant choices to display, aggregate, and/or group the output using the applicable Chart Format drop-downs - Metric, X-Axis Value (choose Standard Balance Flow or Granular Balance Flow), and Metric Aggregation.

Note: The Metric Aggregation option will always be a sum of accounts or balances in this view - the drop-down does not have any other options and is only visible to clarify the value being displayed.

4.    When you are ready, click .

The Balances Waterfall chart and underlying table data are displayed.

5.    Optional: To drill-down and explore the Dimension Migration column, change your Start Date and End Date to reflect only one consecutive time period.
6.    Click .
7.    If there has been movement in the selected dimensions outside of standard balance flows, the Dimension Migration flow column will be blue indicating that you can click it to view drill-down data.

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology
  Refer to the Bank Data Methodology content page for details.
  Definitions
  Balances This metric displays the total end of period balance $ amount of the selected portfolio dimensions or filters.
  Accounts

This metric displays the total accounts of the selected portfolio dimensions.

Note: When grouping by certain dimensions, there is the possibility of a single account appearing twice (e.g., an account may have Switched Out balances to two destinations.

  Customer Acquisition

Customers with new accounts, where existing accounts in other products were not diminished by the starting balance of the new account.

Note: Accounts are generally considered new when balances in the account/product/term in month one are <=0, and balances in month two are >0. For checking accounts we rely on the open date and the appearance of the account in the data to determine whether an account is new.
  Account Acquisition

Balances in new accounts, where existing accounts in other products were not diminished by the starting balance of the new account.

Note: Accounts are generally considered new when balances in the account/product/term in month one are <=0, and balances in month two are >0. For checking accounts we rely on the open date and the appearance of the account in the data to determine whether an account is new.
  Switch In (Switch)

Balances in new accounts that were funded directly from one other or multiple other of the customer’s accounts.
  Switch In (Augmentation)

Augmented balances that are new to the bank in new accounts that were partially funded from one other or multiple other of the customer’s accounts.

Note: Switch In can also occur on an account whose product group, product name, or term changes from month to month.
  Change to Existing

Net augmentation/diminishment for existing accounts.

Note: Existing accounts are generally those accounts that exist with positive balances from one month to the next, and do not change customer ID, account ID, product, term, or line of business. For checking products, an account is treated as an existing account even if the balance is reduced to zero, as long as the account appears in the data and does not have a populated close date.
  Migration (In / Out)

The migration flow represents balance movements that are a result of an intentional back-end re-assignment, driven by the bank or product rules, and not by the customer.  Common examples here include accounts that automatically move to a "standard" product after a certain amount of time from a promotional product, or CDs that automatically migrate to a different term duration upon maturity if no customer action is taken. Migration can be aggregated together in the standard flow, or broken out into "Migration In" or "Migration Out" in the granular flows.
  Switch Out (Switch)

Balances in accounts that have a balance in one month, and in the next month that balance diminishes or attrites and is reflected in the balance of a new account for the same customer.

Note: Generally, when balances in the account/product/term in month one are >0, and balances in month two are <=0, that account is considered closed. For checking accounts we rely on the close date and the appearance of the account in the data to determine whether an account has closed.
  Switch Out (Diminishment) Within accounts that funded another of that customer’s new accounts, the portion of balances that did not appear in the new account, and presumably left the bank.
  Account Attrition Accounts closing where no new account is opened with some or all of the funds.
  Customer Attrition Customers leaving the bank altogether.
  Dimension Migration In certain situations, especially when filters are active, there may be changes in a set of portfolio balances that occur outside of the standard balance flows. This is caused by non "switch" driven re-mapping or migration of various account or customer attributes. A common example is geographic re-mapping. If a customer was in the "New York" market, but has moved to "New Jersey" market - their balances would "disappear" from New York but not due to any traditional balance flow. This discrepancy would be captured in the 'Dimension Migration' flow.  Dimension Migrations can be triggered by any account dimension that is not related to product "switch".

 

Tips, Tricks, & FAQs
  Tips & Tricks  
  Grab a portion of the Balance Waterfall chart to display a smaller range Using your mouse, drag across the area of the visualization for which you want to drill down deeper and view a smaller data segment - in this case, a shorter date range.
  Drag the top and bottom sliders on the Y-axis of the chart to display a smaller range Use the top and/or bottom slider(s) on the Y-axis of the chart to pull down and zoom into a specific balance or account range. On the Balance Waterfall module this can be especially useful to visually drill down into the balance flows themselves, and compare them to one another without the scale skewed by the size o the "start" or "end" balance amounts.
     
  FAQs  
  How do I see the segmentation details of the Dimension Migration value? You must first set a Start Date and End Date that covers only one consecutive time period. For the one-period view, click the Table view, or click the Dimension Migration column to display the segmentation details.
  Why are the segmentation details in the Dimension Migration only available when the date range is set to
1-month? 		Unfortunately, when we try and provide segmentation details for periods greater than one month, customers may essentially create more than one discrepancy that becomes difficult to map across a broader period of time - they change Pricing Markets more than once and potentially move balances to different products, etc...
