Maturing Balances

The Maturing Balances module provides a backwards and forwards looking maturity schedule for term products, as well as important rate and balance metrics regarding maturing term products (APY, reference rates, relative rate of last renewal, APY of balances maturing, etc).

For forward looking maturities, this module analyzes the size and composition of maturity bubbles to provide insights that support effective pricing or product decisions.

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

This module is intended to answer strategic questions like:

  • Are there a large number of maturing accounts (a maturity bubble) coming up? How can I price to retain these customers?

  • What do customers tend to do with their money at maturity? How many of them roll to a new CD, switch to a MMA or other product, or leave the bank?

  • What is the rate paid and reference rate at the last renewal (available from the Table view)?

 

How do I use this Module?

 

The Maturing Balances module provides a past and future maturity schedule for term products, as well as important rate and balance metrics regarding maturing term products (APY, reference rates, relative rate of last renewal, APY of balances maturing, etc). Segment by a variety of variables, including term bucket and term.

1.    Access the Bank Data section (choose Maturing Balances), and use the multi-select Data Filters on the left side to narrow down your institution's unique portfolio segmentation (i.e., Product Group, Term Bucket, etc.).

The default starting selections are All for all Segmentation Filters.

2.    Choose the Start Date and End Date you want to test.

Note: You must click to apply changes.

3.    Select a metric and relevant choices to display, aggregate, and/or group the output using the applicable Chart Format drop-downs - Metric, Group By, Months Displayed, Comparison Metric, and Comparison Value.

This organizes your data for different views - and each view tells a different story.

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
  Refer to the Bank Data Methodology for details.
  Data Dictionary
  Reference Rates External reference rates that are aggregations of peer advertised pricing. Click here to see in-scope reference rates, and their definitions.
  % of First Maturity Percent of balances in the aggregated cell that are made up of term accounts that have not yet had a maturity event since they were first opened, and are thus considered to be at 'first maturity'.
  CD Renewal Status/ Renewal Flag Used to describe if a term product has experienced a maturity event since account opening or is still at 'first maturity.' Depending on your configuration, there may be multiple available CD Renewal Statuses.
  Absolute Rate The Absolute Rate is the weighted value of the selected external reference rate across the cells selected for the analyzes
  Relative Rate The Relative Rate is the ratio between the Reference Rate and your institution rate.

 

Tips, Tricks, & FAQs
  Tips & Tricks  
  In this module, the Table View offers more detailed data than is visible in the chart Click the Table tab to view the rate paid and reference rate at the last renewal.
  Grab a portion of the Total Balances chart to display a smaller date range Using your mouse, drag across the area of the visualization for which you want to drill down deeper and view a smaller data segment - in this case, a shorter date range.
  If you have clicked and dragged the visualization to drill down to a select range - click Reset Axes to expand back out to the original chart view.
