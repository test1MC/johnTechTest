Switch Balances

The Switch Balances module provides a dynamic view of customer level switch analysis identifying flow of balances both to different products within the same product group, as well as across product groups. It is a drill down from the Balance Flows module, providing both the from and to segmentations of switched balances (Switch Out and Switch In, respectively).

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

  • Track the flow of funds out of a product (e.g., Money Market) by selecting it as the Switch Out Product Group and then choosing a Group By selection of Switch-In Product to show where balances have moved over time.
  • Track augmentation vs. cannibalization filtering by both Switch Out and Switch In and then Group By by balance flow.

 

How do I use this Module?

 

Use the Switch Balances module to view customer level switch analysis that identifies flow of balances between existing and new accounts within the bank.

1.    Access the Bank Data section (choose Switch Balances), and use the multi-select Data Filters on the left side to narrow down your institution's unique portfolio segmentation (i.e., SO Product Group, SO Term Bucket, etc.).

The default starting selections are All for all Segmentation Filters.

2.    Choose the Dataset (Monthly or Week over Week) and enter the Start Date and End Date you want to test.

Note: You must click to apply changes.

3.    Optional: Click the Switch In tab on the left side to include Switch In filters as- click the Switch Out and Switch In tabs to move back and forth between those two Data Filter sets.
4.    Select a metric and relevant choices to display, aggregate, and/or group the output using the applicable Chart Format drop-downs - X-Axis Value, Group By , Metric, and Metric Aggregation.

This organizes your data for different views - and each view tells a different story.

Nuances to this module

  • This module is a little different in that you will want to choose the scope for both Switch Out and Switch In filters - click the Switch Out and Switch In tabs to move back and forth between Data Filters
  • Use the Hide Model Exclusions checkbox to remove outliers for better clarity. Hover over these drop-down in the above screenshot for full details.
  • From the Legend on the right side of the page, toggle on and off any of the metrics displayed. Click All to display all or None to turn off all of the metrics (and then turn back on one at a time).
  • Toggle between the Value and Percent radio buttons.
  • Click the Table tab to view the underlying data.

 

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
  Refer to the Bank Data Methodology for details.
  Definitions  
  Switch Balance

Balances identified as starting out in the Switch Out account and ending up in the Switch In account.

  Switch In Balance w/ Augmentation

Augmented balances that are new to the bank in new accounts that were partially funded from one other or multiple other of the customer’s accounts. For example, if $10,000 leaves a Money Market account to become a $15,000 CD, and no other customer accounts were diminished to fund the CD, $5,000 would be considered Switch In Augmentation.

Note: Switch In can also occur on an account whose Product Group, Product Name, or Term changes from month to month.
  Switch Out Balance w/ Diminishment

Within accounts that funded another of that customer’s new accounts- this is the portion of balances that did not appear in the new account and presumably left the bank. For example, if a $10,000 CD closed, and only $7,000 made it into another account, $3,000 would be considered Switch Out Diminishment.
  Switch In Rate The weighted rate paid on the destination (Switch In) accounts
  Switch Out Rate The weighted rate paid on the source (Switch Out) accounts
  Switch Accounts The number of accounts with switch behavior based on the active Segmentation Filters and Chart Format selections.
