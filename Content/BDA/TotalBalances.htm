Total Balances

The Total Balances module allows you to analyze trends in period end account data across a variety of segmentations. Track recent balance, account, or rate movements, and identify potential problems, such as a shift in product mix or a decline in a particular market.

Enter the Dataset (e.g., Monthly, Weekly), as well as the Start Date and End Date you want to test.

Choose the metric value to be displayed up the Y-Axis (refer to the Methodology and Definitions table below for details).

Choose either Time (i.e., the selected date range) or an institutional data segment (e.g., Pricing Market).

Choose how to segment the data up the vertical Y-axis (e.g., choose Product Group to segment data by Checking, Savings, CD, Money Market, etc). Choose None to combine all data into one value.

Note: Each segmentation is displayed in the Chart legend and metric details are displayed on the Table tab.

Choose how data is summarized. Available options:

Sum: The only option available when viewing data with Time on the X-Axis. Represents the metric's actual value for each date shown.

Monthly Average The straight average of the selected metric over the total number of months between the start date and end date.

Average Over Observations: The straight average of the selected metric over its total number of occurrences in the data.

Weighted Average: This applies to calculated values. The aggregate value is a calculated as a balance weighted average across months.

Click the Table Tab to display the hover-over metrics from the chart in a table.

Click the Hide Model Exclusions checkbox to prevent any acknowledged outliers from being displayed in the data (set up per institution).

The Group Insignificant Items checkbox aggregates insignificant items int he chart and table to a single Other bucket, to keep the output cleaner and easier to read in situations with multiple but insignificant data elements in the Group By selection.

Hover over a column to display the metrics displayed in the table.

Click Update View to display the chart and table after you have made your Chart Format selections (e.g., Metric, X-Axis Value, Group By and Metric Aggregation).

Click the Percent radio button to display the Y Axis metric as a percentage of 100.

From the Chart key, toggle on or off any of the metrics displayed. Click All to display all or None to turn off all of the metrics.

 

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

  • Analyze trends across a variety of segmentations (e.g., over a data period, track total balances against a particular product like Money Market balances to determine trends).
  • Track potential problems such as a decline in a particular market, determining trends in certain markets.?

 

How do I use this Module?

 

The Total Balances module allows you to analyze trends in period end stock account balance data across a variety of segmentations. Track recent balance, account, or rate movements, and identify potential problems, such as a shift in product mix or a decline in a particular markets.

1.    Access the Bank Data section (choose Total Balances), and use the multi-select Data Filters on the left side to narrow down your institution's unique portfolio segmentation (i.e., Product Group, Term Bucket, etc.).

The default starting selections are All for all Segmentation Filters.

2.    Choose the Dataset (Monthly or Week over Week) and enter the Start Date and End Date you want to test.

Note: You must click to apply changes.

3.    Select a metric and relevant choices to display, aggregate, and/or group the output using the applicable Chart Format drop-downs - Metric, X-Axis Value, Group By, and Metric Aggregation.

This organizes your data for different views - and each view tells a different story.

4.    When you are ready, click .

Nuances to this module

  • Use the Hide Model Exclusions and Group Insignificant Items checkboxes to remove outliers and group items for better clarity. Hover over these drop-down in the above screenshot for full details.
  • From the Legend on the right side of the page, toggle on and off any of the metrics displayed. Click All to display all or None to turn off all of the metrics (and then turn back on one at a time).
  • Toggle between the Value and Percent radio buttons.
  • Click the Table tab to view the underlying data.

 

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
  Refer to the Bank Data Methodology for details.
  Data Dictionary
  Balances This metric displays the total end of period balance $ amount of the selected portfolio dimensions or filters.
  Accounts

This metric displays the total accounts of the selected portfolio dimensions.

Note: When grouping by certain dimensions, there is the possibility of a single account appearing twice (e.g., an account may have Switched Out balances to two destinations.

  Accts/Branch This metric displays the average number of accounts in each branch for the selected portfolio dimensions.
  Average Bals/Acct This metric displays the average total balance $ amount in each account.
  Bals/Branch This metric displays the average total balance $ amount per branch. Effectively total balances divided by the number of branches they are sourced from based on active Segmentation Filters.
  Rate and Accts

The weighted average offered interest rate within the selected portfolio dimensions. The total number of accounts will be reflected across the Group By dimension as a bar chart, and the overall weighted rate will be overlaid as a line chart.

Click the Table tab to display both metrics in a table.
  Rate and Bals

This metric displays the average offered weighted interest rate. The balance values will be reflected across the Group By dimension as a bar chart, and the overall weighted rate will be overlaid as a line chart.

Click the Table radio button to display both metrics in a table.
  Weighted Rate This metric displays the average weighted rate as a line graph. The Weighted Rate measures the annual rate of the accounts as of the period end date and is not calculated based on interest dollars paid.

 

Tips, Tricks, & FAQs
  Tips & Tricks  
  Grab a portion of the Total Balances chart to display a smaller date range Using your mouse, drag across the area of the visualization for which you want to drill down deeper and view a smaller data segment - in this case, a shorter date range.
  If you have clicked and dragged the visualization to drill down to a select range - click Reset Axes to expand back out to the original chart view.
