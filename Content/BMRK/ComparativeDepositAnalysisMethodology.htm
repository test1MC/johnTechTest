CDA Introduction

 

Comparative Deposit Analytics (CDA) delivers comprehensive deposit metric benchmarks in a timely and regular fashion.

 

CDA Overview

 

Guaranteeing Anonymity

Novantas takes the anonymity of the data incredibly seriously - we utilize a minimum of four banks in all peer sets, with straight averages, to ensure no individual bank's information can be derived from CDA reports

 

Consistent Product Definitions

Because CDA is built from account and customer level balances of well known portfolios, we are able to apply very consistent product definitions.

Note: Only consumer balances are currently in scope (Private client or wealth balances are excluded.

  • The following products are excluded:
    • Tax advantaged accounts (e.g. HSA, IRA) — rates may not be similar to market rates, and performance is often unrelated to rate.
    • Indexed rate products — rates and performance may not be reflective of bank actions.
    • Trust accounts (e.g. IOLTA, IORTA) — performance may not be rate driven.
  • However, non-traditional, non-IRA CDs are included (e.g. bump rate, step rate, callable).
  • Balances in scope for CDA are mapped to four broad product groups:
    • Non-Interest Checking — non-interest bearing checking products
    • Interest Checking — interest bearing checking products
    • Savings — all non-checking liquid accounts; includes accounts categorized as high yield savings, passbook savings, and money market
    • CDs — term products
  • Additionally, further groupings of the above four product groups may be available on certain dashboards:
    • All Products — all products in CDA (non-interest checking, interest checking, savings, and CDs)
    • Liquid — non-maturity deposits (non-interest checking, interest checking, savings)
    • All Checking — non-interest checking and interest checking
