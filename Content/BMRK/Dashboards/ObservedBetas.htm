Observed Betas

The Observed Betas Dashboard displays rates, rate changes, and betas compared to Fed Funds for a variety of products, for acquisition as well as for the whole portfolio.

Hover your mouse over the screenshot below to display pop-up content detailing sections of the screen:

Use the drop-down arrows to change the data set.

  • The Institution drop-down allows you to choose the subset of institution for comparison (bank or peer sets). You can choose multiple comparative data sets (*new*) - however, the dashboard display is not optimized for viewing too many peer sets at once. Selecting too many peer sets may force data off the chart.

Note: For details on the institutions that make up each data set, click the Reporting drop-down and choose Data Pulls. Download the CDA Peers report.

Slide the Date Range slidebar to narrow the date range. This controls the date range for both the beta/rate change calculations on the top two charts as well as the two time series charts on the bottom.

Note: If you do not see data for the most recent month for comparative data sets (e.g., Large Banks, Mid-Size Peers), it is because that data is not yet available. Use the Slider to pull back to the prior month until data is displayed.

View either the Acquisition and Portfolio Rate Change charts - or the Acquisition and Portfolio Beta charts (where Beta equals the percent of the Federal Funds rate change passed through to the product rate).

Use the drop-down arrows to change the data set.

  • The Product drop-down allows you to choose rate sensitive products (e.g., savings accounts, checking, CDs).

Note: This selection only affects the top two charts. The bottom two charts always show All Products in a time series view of the rates against the Fed Funds rate for both your institution and your comparative Peer Sets.

  • The Term Buck drop-down allows you to drill-down further into Term Buckets (e.g., 0-23 months, 24+ months) for CDs. This filter is dependent on the Product filter.

Note: For Products without terms (e.g., Checking, Savings), the default is NA (Not Applicable).

  • The Rate Buck drop-down allows you to drill-down further into Rate Buckets (e.g., 0-24 bps, 0-49 bps, 0-99 bps, 25+ bps). The default is All. The default is All. Use this filter to view high yield savings vs. low yield savings performance.

Note: Selection options are not mutually exclusive - as they are for Term Buckets.

  • The Balance Tier drop-down allows you to see only certain balances (e.g., $0-$99,999, $100,000+).

Hover over any of the bars in either of the two charts to display the comparative graph for that product (refer to How do I use this Module for a screenshot).

Download the report as a PDF, Image, or Data (in CSV format, which can be open in Excel easily or pulled into a database app).

Note: Since there are multiple visualizations present, the last visualization accessed is the one downloaded. To download alternate visualizations, first click it to select before downloading.

 

Common Use Cases for this Module
  • Are my rate betas higher or lower than that of my peers? Does this differ by acquisition rates vs. portfolio rates?

  • My portfolio betas are higher than peers; which product is that driven by?

  • How have rate betas changed over time and for different Fed moves? Are betas trending higher more recently?

 

How do I use this Module?

 

1.    From the Benchmarking header, choose Analytics.

The CDA Workbook page is displayed.

2.    Click the 3a. Observed Betas dashboard.
3.    Optional: Use the Institution drop-down arrow to change the peer set (e.g., all Banks, Online, Promo Heavy MMDA Regionals).

Tip: The default is All, and this will likely result in a display error. De-select All and choose - ideally - two comparative data sets. For details on the institutions that make up each data set, click the Reporting drop-down and choose Data Pulls. Download the CDA Peers report.

4.    Optional: Use the Product drop-down arrow to change the product (e.g., Savings, Short and Long-term CDs) for the top two charts.
5.    Optional: Use the Term Buck drop-down arrow to drill-down further into Term Buckets (e.g., 0-23 months, 24+ months) for those products to which it relates (e.g., CDs) - also for the top two charts only.

Note: For Products without terms (e.g., Checking, Savings), the default is NA (Not Applicable).

6.    Optional: Use the Rate Buck drop-down arrow to drill-down further into Rate Buckets (e.g., 0-24 bps, 0-49 bps, 0-99 bps, 25+ bps).

The default is All.

7.    Optional: Use the Balance Tier drop-down arrow to see only certain balances (e.g., $0-$99,999, $100,000+).
8.    Slide the Date Range slidebar to narrow the date range. This controls the date range for both the beta/rate change calculations.

Note: If you do not see data for the most recent month for comparative data sets (e.g., Large Banks, Mid-Size Peers), it is because that data is not yet available. Use the Slider to pull back to the prior month until data is displayed.

9.    Optional: Click the Beta or Rate Change drop-down to view either the Acquisition and Portfolio Rate Change charts - or the Acquisition and Portfolio Beta charts (where Beta equals the percent of the Federal Funds rate change passed through to the product rate).
10.    Optional: Hover over any of the bars in either of the two charts to display the comparative graph for that product. The Fed Funds Target Rate is shown via the black line:

 

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
  Refer to the Comparative Deposit Analysis (CDA) Methodology for details.
  Data Dictionary
   
Betas

The percent of each macro rate change passed through to the product rate.

  • For example, if over a specified time period the Fed Funds rate increased by 50bp and a bank's MMA product increased by 20bp, that product would have a 40% beta (entered as .4 in PriceTek's Scenario or Optimization workflows).
