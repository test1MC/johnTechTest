Performance by Vintage

The Performance by Vintage Dashboard breaks up the current portfolio balances into when they were acquired and shows metrics by account age (tenure).

Hover your mouse over the screenshot below to display pop-up content detailing sections of the screen:

Hover over for a description.Hover over for a description.Hover over for a description.Hover over for a description.

Use the drop-down arrows to change the data set.

  • The Institution drop-down allows you to choose the subset of institution for comparison (bank or peer sets). You can choose multiple comparative data sets (*new*) - however, the dashboard display is not optimized for viewing too many peer sets at once. Selecting too many peer sets may force data off the chart.

Note: For details on the institutions that make up each data set, click the Reporting drop-down and choose Data Pulls. Download the CDA Peers report.

  • The Product drop-down allows you to choose - only liquid products are included for vintage dashboards (i.e., All Checking, All Liquid, Interest Checking, Non-Interest Checking, and Savings).
  • The Date drop-down allows you to choose a month-end date. If you do not have acceptable comparable institutions for the latest month-end, go back one month as comparative data may lag one month behind.

The Blue line indicates your bank while other colors reflect the comparative data sets. Use the Legend for reference.

Hover over any of the bars to display full metrics for the selected tenure. For example, 66% of accounts acquired between 6 and 11 months ago remain.

Each account tenure bucket reflects accounts acquired in the data range prior to the Date selected (e.g., accounts in the 12 – 23 Months bucket were acquired from 11/30/2017 to 8/31/2018, as calculated from 8/31/2019).

 

Common Use Cases for this Module
  • Vintage Performance: How are my longer tenured, lower rate accounts performing relative to peers? Are they declining in balances?
  • Am I paying more on longer tenured accounts than peers?
  • How does my change in mix compare to peers?

 

How do I use this Module?

 

1.    From the Benchmarking header, choose Analytics.

The CDA Workbook page is displayed.

2.    Click the 5e. Performance by Vintage dashboard.

Bar charts are displayed in four different categories (% accounts remaining, % balances remaining, balances per account, and weighted rate) by account tenure.

3.    Optional: Use the Institution drop-down arrow to change the peer set (e.g., all Banks, Online, Promo Heavy MMDA Regionals).
4.    Optional: Click the Product drop-down arrow and choose a bank product to drill down and display comparative metrics for a specific product.

Only liquid products are included for vintage dashboards (i.e., All Checking, All Liquid, Interest Checking, Non-Interest Checking, and Savings).

5.    Optional: Use the Date drop-down allows you to choose a month-end date

Tip: If you do not have acceptable comparable institutions for the latest month-end, go back one month as comparative data may lag one month behind.

6.    Hover over bar to view metric details for the selected account tenure.

 

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
  Refer to the Comparative Deposit Analysis (CDA) Methodology for details.
  Data Dictionary
  % Acct Remaining The percent of acquired accounts that are currently remaining; calculated as the number of accounts currently remaining divided by the number of accounts acquired in month 0.
  % Balances Remaining The percent of acquired balances that are currently remaining; calculated as the total balances currently remaining divided by the total balances acquired in month 0.
  % Monthly Balance Change The monthly balance change on the accounts; calculated as the difference between this month’s balances and last month’s balances, divided by last month’s balances .
  Weighted Rate The weighted average rate, calculated as the total interest expense divided by total balances.

  Choosing Institutions

When you initially access this dashboard, the default is All Institutions are displayed, and this will likely result in a display error. De-select All and choose - ideally - two comparative data sets.

For details on the institutions that make up each data set, click the Reporting drop-down and choose Data Pulls. Download the CDA Peers report.
