Summary Metrics

The Summary Metrics Dashboard provides an overview of key metrics covering portfolio composition, flow of funds, rate distribution, and time series balance growth.

Hover your mouse over the screenshot below to display pop-up content detailing sections of the screen:

Hover over for a definition.Hover over for a definition.Hover over for a definition.Hover over for a definition.

Use the drop-down arrows to change the data set.

  • The Institution drop-down allows you to choose the subset of institution for comparison (bank or peer sets). You can choose multiple comparative data sets (*new*) - however, the dashboard display is not optimized for viewing too many peer sets at once. Selecting too many peer sets may force data off the chart.

Note: For details on the institutions that make up each data set, click the Reporting drop-down and choose Data Pulls. Download the CDA Peers report.

  • The Product drop-down allows you to choose any institutional product (e.g., savings accounts, checking, CDs).
  • The Term Buck drop-down allows you to drill-down further into Term Buckets (e.g., 0-23 months, 24+ months) for CDs. This filter is dependent on the Product filter.

Note: For Products without terms (e.g., Checking, Savings), the default is NA (Not Applicable).

  • The Rate Buck drop-down allows you to drill-down further into Rate Buckets (e.g., 0-24 bps, 0-49 bps, 0-99 bps, 25+ bps). The default is All. The default is All. Use this filter to view high yield savings vs. low yield savings performance.

Note: Selection options are not mutually exclusive - as they are for Term Buckets.

  • The Balance Tier drop-down allows you to see only certain balances (e.g., $0-$99,999, $100,000+).
  • The Date Range Type drop-down allows you to choose the date ranges to display in the table column(s): Last 3 Months, Last 12 Months, Last Month, and/or Year to Date. Just as with the Institution drop-down, if you toggle on too many ranges, some data may be forced off the chart.
  • The Metric Type drop-down allows you to choose between Percent and Per Branch values.

Click any single metric from the chart (e.g., % Balance Acquisition Growth) to display the historic metrics in the graph on the right (refer to How do I use this screen? below for details).

Blue text indicates your bank while other colors reflect the comparative data sets. Use the Legend for reference.

Download the report as a PDF, Image, or Data (in CSV format, which can be open in Excel easily or pulled into a database app).

Note: Since there are multiple visualizations present, the last visualization accessed is the one downloaded. To download alternate visualizations, first click it to select before downloading.

 

Common Use Cases for this Module
  • Industry Trends in the cost of Deposits: What are front-book and back-book betas?
  • Industry Trends in Balance Growth: Are deposits growing or shrinking for the industry? Which products are growing vs. shrinking?
  • Front-Book Pricing: Am I overpaying for a similar balance growth? Is my “all in” cost of acquiring dollars higher or lower than peers?
  • Back-Book Management: How do my savings retention or CD renewal rates compare to peers? How do my back-book savings rates compare with that of peers?
  • Are peer banks growing faster or slower than I am? How has growth varied in the past?
  • Is attrition increasing for peers?
  • Do peer banks have a higher or lower weighted rate than I do?
  • As the rate environment is shifting, are peers experiencing a lot of shift into term balances? Any increases in switch into higher rate products?

 

How do I use this Module?

 

1.    From the Benchmarking header, choose Analytics.

The CDA Workbook page is displayed.

2.    Click the 1a. Summary Metrics dashboard.

A list of Metric Categories (e.g., Balance Growth Waterfall) are displayed with the latest month end and 12-month metrics displayed.

3.    Optional: Use the Institution drop-down arrow to change the peer set (e.g., all Banks, Online, Promo Heavy MMDA Regionals).

Tip: The default is All, and this will likely result in a display error. De-select All and choose - ideally - two comparative data sets. For details on the institutions that make up each data set, click the Reporting drop-down and choose Data Pulls. Download the CDA Peers report.

4.    Optional: Use the Product drop-down arrow to change the product (e.g., Savings, Short and Long-term CDs).
5.    Optional: Use the Term Buck drop-down arrow to drill-down further into Term Buckets (e.g., 0-23 months, 24+ months) for those products to which it relates (e.g., CDs).

Note: For Products without terms (e.g., Checking, Savings), the default is NA (Not Applicable).

6.    Optional: Use the Rate Buck drop-down arrow to drill-down further into Rate Buckets (e.g., 0-24 bps, 0-49 bps, 0-99 bps, 25+ bps).

The default is All.

7.    Optional: Use the Balance Tier drop-down arrow to see only certain balances (e.g., $0-$99,999, $100,000+).
8.    Optional: Use the Date Range Type drop-down arrow to choose the date ranges to display in the table column(s): Last 3 Months, Last 12 Months, Last Month, and/or Year to Date.

Tip: You can choose multiple selections, but just as with the Institution drop-down, the dashboard may error out if you toggle on too many ranges.

9.    Click any single metric from the table (e.g., Average Balances per Acquired Account).

Historic metrics for that metric are displayed on the right side of the page:

10.    Hover over anywhere in the graph to display the metrics for any month across time.
