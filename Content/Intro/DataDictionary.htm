Data Dictionary

This table compiles the Data Dictionary definitions listed throughout this Help document.

  Acquisition

Balances in new accounts, where existing accounts in other products were not diminished by the starting balance of the new account.

Note: Accounts are generally considered new when balances in the account/product/term in month one are <=0, and balances in month two are >0. For checking accounts we generally rely on the open date and the appearance of the account in the data to determine whether an account is new.  Click here to see balance flow configuration exceptions unique to your institution.
  Area of Indifference Line On an elasticity chart - indicates portions of the Fitted Model that demonstrate insignificant slope relative to the other areas of the model (i.e., areas where a change in relative rate in either direction results in minimal change to the dependent variable). Depending on what is selected for Color Data Points , values will show up for each distinct dimension.
  Attrition Either customers leaving the bank all together, or accounts closing where no new account is opened with some or all of the funds.
  Beta

The percent of each macro rate change passed through to the product rate.

  • For example, if over a specified time period the Fed Funds rate increased by 50bp and a bank's MMA product increased by 20bp, that product would have a 40% beta (entered as .4 in PriceTek's Scenario or Optimization workflows)
  BPS Basis point (BPS) refers to a common unit of measure for interest rates and other percentages in finance. One basis point is equal to 1/100th of 1%, or 0.01%, or 0.0001.
  BPS Difference You are choosing to enter a forecasted rate difference between the selected competitors by basis points. One basis point is equal to 1/100th of 1%, or 0.01%, or 0.0001, and is used to denote the percentage change in a financial instrument. The relationship between percentage changes and basis points can be summarized as follows: 1% change = 100 basis points, and 0.01% = 1 basis point.
  Change to Existing

Net augmentation/diminishment for existing accounts.

Note: Existing accounts are generally those accounts that exist with positive balances from one month to the next, and do not change customer ID, account ID, product, term, or line of business. For checking products, an account is treated as an existing account even if the balance is reduced to zero, as long as the account appears in the data and does not have a populated close date.
  Fitted Model Line Indicates a modeled relationship between the in-scope actual datapoints on a chart and relevant independent variables.
  Lag

The gap in time between a macro change and a product rate change.

  • For example, if the Fed Funds rate increases in December and the lag is set to 1, the response is effective in January.
  • Lags can be set to 0, 1 or 2 months.
  Lock Current Differences When creating a Static Assumption Rule (in the Optimization module): Lock the basis point (bp) difference between adjacent segments in the selected dimension. If Segment A is priced at 1.00% and Segment B is priced at 1.50%, this rule would ensure that Segments A and B are priced in lock step where Segment B is always priced exactly 50 bp above Segment A.
  Lock Current Ordering When creating a Static Assumption Rule (in the Optimization module): Maintain the order in which segments in the selected dimension are priced. If Segment A is priced at 1.00% and Segment B is priced at 1.50%, this rule would ensure that Segment B is priced above Segment A by at least 1 bp.
  Maximize Average Balance When setting an Optimization Target (in the Optimization module): Maximize the average End Balance across all months.
  Maximize End Balance When setting an Optimization Target (in the Optimization module): Maximize the End Balance for the final month of the duration.
  Maximize Month 1 Acquisition When setting an Optimization Target (in the Optimization module): Maximize acquisition balances in the first month.
  Maximize Total Revenue When setting an Optimization Target (in the Optimization module): Maximize the sum of the Monthly Spread Revenue ([FTP - Rate]+ / 12 * Balance) across all months). FTP is the acronym for Funds Transfer Price
  Minimize End Weighted Rate When setting an Optimization Target (in the Optimization module): Minimize the Weighted Rate in the final month of the duration.
  Minimize Total Interest Expense When setting an Optimization Target (in the Optimization module): Minimize the sum of the Monthly Interest Expense (Rate / 12 * Balance) across all months.
  Percent Attrite The % of balances that leave the bank altogether.
  Percent Change Stay The % of balances that stay with the institution, the month-over-month percent change in balances.
  Percent Difference A rate difference by % change.
  Percent Roll The % of starting balance that partially or entirely stays with the bank (or does not attrite).
  Percent Stay The % of balances that stay within the bank, and stay within their original term.
  Percent Switch The % of balances that stay with the bank but switch to another product/term.
  Roll The amount that partially or entirely stays with the bank (does not attrite) upon maturity.
  Static Assumption Value Any Static Assumption will override the curve with a flat line - the projection will be set to the Value from the rule at all relative rates.
  Switch In (Augmentation)

Augmented balances that are new to the bank in new accounts that were partially funded from one other or multiple other of the customer’s accounts.

Note: Switch In can also occur on an account whose product group, product name, or term changes from month to month.
  Switch In (Switch) Balances in new accounts that were funded directly from one other or multiple other of the customer’s accounts.
  Switch Out (Diminishment) Within accounts that funded another of that customer’s new accounts, the portion of balances that did not appear in the new account, and presumably left the bank.
  Switch Out (Switch)

Balances in accounts that have a balance in one month, and in the next month that balance diminishes or attrites and is reflected in the balance of a new account for the same customer.

Note: Generally, when balances in the account/product/term in month one are >0, and balances in month two are <=0, that account is considered closed. For checking accounts we rely on the close date and the appearance of the account in the data to determine whether an account has closed.
  WAMP

The Weighted Average Market Price is the weighted average of all tracked brick and mortar banks

Click here to see in-scope reference rates specific to your institution, and their definitions.

  Weighted Max

This metric is dependent on whether your institution is defined as large bank, small bank , or direct bank.

Click here to see in-scope reference rates specific to your institution, and their definitions.

  Weighted Rate This metric displays the average weighted rate within the selected Product Group (e.g., CDs, All) and maps it as a line graph on the Y-Axis against the X-Axis Value (e.g., Pricing Market). It displays the total balance $ amount within the selected Product Group as a bar chart. Click the Table radio button to display both metrics in a table.   Weighted Average This applies to calculated values. The aggregate value is a calculated as a balance weighted average across months.
