How To Answer These Business Questions

PriceTek is intended to answer a wide variety of business questions related to banking deposits. You can often get lost finding answers to those questions within eHelp as the focus is on how to use each module. This page intends to focus on how to leverage the PriceTek product itself to answer those questions.

Note: To submit a question you would like explored on this page, please email pricetek_support@novantas.com.

 

How do I evaluate the performance of a recent campaign(s)?

 

To evaluate the growth associated with a recent campaign, use the Balance Flows module from the Bank Data section to check for new money growth in the region(s) and time periods in which the campaign was run.

1.    On the Balance Flows module from the Bank Data section, use the Data Filters on the left side to narrow down the portfolio segmentation:
A.   Choose a date range starting a month or two before a campaign began and at least a few months after (e.g., if a campaign was run from March 2018 to June 2018, then you would want to choose a date range of January 2018 until August or September 2018).
B.   Choose the Product of the campaign and any other identifying dimensions such as Segments targeted, Pricing Regions, etc.
2.    Above the data chart, set the Chart Format drop-down options:
A.   To focus on acquisition dollars, change the Metric to Balance Change.
B.   Set the X-Axis Value to Time.
C.   From the Balance Flows selection box, click only the Acquisition checkbox.

Note: To filter by Acquisition sub-categories (e.g., Customer Acquisition, Account Acquisition, Switch-In Augmentation), select the Granular radio button in the top right of the Balance Flows selection box and the click the Balance Flows selection check boxes that are relevant for you.

3.    Click the Update View button.

What to look for:

  • Assess the acquisition difference between the months preceding the campaign and the months following the campaign. For the example above, that would mean assessing the difference in acquisition between February 2018 and the start of the campaign in March 2018 (roughly $125mm) as well as the difference in acquisition between the end of the campaign in June 2018 and the following month, July 2018 (also about $125mm). Click the Table tab to access the raw data (which you can download into Excel).

Further analysis suggestions:

  • Explore other Metrics for different views on the campaign, suggestions include:
    • End Rate: To see the rates at which balances are being acquired, choose End Rate and then from the Group-By drop-down, group by Tier Bucket, Pricing Region or Pricing Market, Segment, etc.. to review differentiation across dimensions.
    • Account change: To view account growth instead of dollar growth.
  • To identify how much money switched into the accounts promoted in the campaign from existing balances, click the Balance Flows drop-down and de-select Acquisition and instead, choose Switch In. If the switch in value is significant, then use the Switch Balances module to drill into cannibalization.
    • For more information, refer to our Investigating Cannibalization 90 second demo.

 

How do I use PriceTek to drill into cannibalization?

 

Use the Bank Data section to track internal movement in your portfolio. In this example, we’ll use the Switch Balances module to walk through evaluating cannibalization following a recent rate reduction.

1.    On the Switch Balances module from the Bank Data section, use the Data Filters on the left side to narrow down the portfolio segmentation:
A.   In this module, there are two sets of Data Filters on the left side – a set for Switch In dimensions, and a set for Switch Out dimensions- since we are looking at a rate reduction, we’ll start by selecting the Switch Out tab, and then using the Switch Out Data Filters to limit to the Product (e.g. Money Market) and identifying dimensions where rates were changed.

This limits our analysis to balances that switched out of the product with a reduction.

B.   Then, choose a date range a month or two before a rate change began and at least a few months after (e.g., if a rate shift was made on March 2018, then you would want to choose a date range of January 2018 until May or June 2018.
2.    Above the data chart, set the Chart Format drop-down options:
A.   To focus on the actual switch balances for the dates that coincide with the rate change, click the Group By drop-down and choose Switch-In Product Group.

This will display the money that switched out of the selected Product (e.g., Monkey Market) grouped by the Switch-in Product.

B.   Click the Metric drop-down and choose Switch Balance.
3.    Click the Update View button.

What to look for:

  • From here, your choices are dependent on the findings, and where you would like to drill down to more granularity. For this example, Switch-In amounts are mostly going to other Money Market accounts, but a about 33% are switching to CDs. To investigate what duration CDs are pulling the balances, click the Switch In tab for Data Filters on the left side, and then from the SI Product Group drop-down choose CDs.

Further analysis suggestions:

  • To view the weighted average from/to rates, respectively, for your analysis – and what the impact is on your cost of funds, change the Metric drop-down to either Switch Out Rate or Switch In Rate.
  • Use the Balance Flows module to analyze switch out as a percentage of total balances to analyze the scale impact of balance movements: 
    • Set the Metric drop-down to % of Start Balances, the X-Axis Value as Time, and then to focus on Switch Out dollars, click the Balance Flows drop-down and de-select everything except Switch Out.
    • Look for dips that correspond with the time periods in question:

  • For more information on how to use the Balance Flows module with the Switch Balances module to drill into cannibalization, refer to our Investigating Cannibalization 90 second demo.

 

How can I measure attrition across portfolio segments?

 

Attrition can be measured in accounts, dollars, or as a percentage of starting balances in the Balance Flows module from the Bank Data section. In this example, we’ll investigate attrition using a number of different drill-downs available in the Balance Flows module – examining the difference in attrition across a selected portfolio dimension.

Note: Attrition can also be viewed and measured over time via the Balance Waterfall module.
1.    On the Balance Flows module from the Bank Data section, use the Data Filters on the left side to narrow down the portfolio segmentation:
A.   It is recommended that initially you choose a wide date range to look for spikes and/or trends in attrition (e.g., May 2016 to present).
B.   Choose the Product and any other identifying dimensions such as Segments, Pricing Regions, etc.
2.    Above the data chart, set the Chart Format drop-down options:
A.   To focus on attrition dollars, change the Metric to Balance Change.
B.   Set the X-Axis Value to Time.
C.   From the Balance Flows selection box, click only the Attrition checkbox.

Note: To filter by Attrition sub-categories (i,e., Customer Attrition, Account Attrition, and Switch Out (Dim)), select the Granular radio button in the top right of the Balance Flows selection box and the click the Balance Flows selection check boxes that are relevant for you.

3.    Click the Update View button.
4.    Look for spikes or areas of unusual attrition to investigate.

In the screenshot to the right, the spike in CD attrition in March of 2017 stands out - so let's hone in on CDs in that time period for the rest of our analysis.

5.    With a select portfolio segment and time period in mind to investigate (and staying on the Balance Flows module), tweak the Data Filters on the left side. It is recommended to keep a wide date range to be able to better assess trends and/or anomalies (e.g., May 2016 to present), and let's focus on CDs as the Product Group.
6.    Above the data chart, set the Chart Format drop-down options:
A.   Change the Metric to % of Start Balances.

This will return attrition relative to starting balances, which can help eliminate misleading “spikes” from portfolio segments with substantially large balance volumes overall. Look for any dips in the line bar in the graph over the selected date range. A dip indicates an increase in attrition.

B.   Keep the X-Axis Value value set to Time and the Balance Flows value set to Attrition.
C.   Use the Group By drop-down and choose the dimension that you want to explore. In this example, we are examining Pricing Region to dig further into attrition as a percentage of starting balances.
7.    Click the Update View button.

Note: To reduce the “noise” in your chart, you can de-select dimensions from the Legend on the right. In the example above, we have de-selected Unmapped geographies). You can also use the Hide Model Exclusions checkbox to remove outliers and group items for better clarity.

8.    From this view, you can explore which Pricing Regions, Product Names, Term Buckets, etc.. may have caused the dip (in our case, for March 2017), indicating an increase in attrition.

In the screenshot above, we have found using the Group By selection that the spike in CD attrition runs across all Pricing Regions. In the screenshot below, we have found using a Group By selection of Term Bucket that the spike in CD attrition seems to have been disproportionally due to attrition in the 10-15 month term bucket which we would then want to examine further (e.g., look at the Rates of various terms).

What to look for:

  • Logically, if/when you see an increase in attrition at a particular date, you can explore whether competitor actions, advertising campaigns, or even customers withdrawing money to potentially pay their taxes explains the attrition. If you have our Comparative Analytics data (the CDA section), you can use the Balance Flows Over Time report to investigate competitors market share per product).

Further analysis suggestions:

  • Use all three Attrition sub-categories (i,e., Customer Attrition, Account Attrition, and Switch Out (Dim)) on the Balance Flows selection box that are available once you toggle on the Granular radio button. Then, click the Group By drop-down and choose Flow Group. This view allows you to determine if your attrition is driven mostly by lost customers, lost accounts of customers that remain, or diminishment of balances in accounts that remain.
  • Visit the Balance Waterfall module to analyze the overall impact of attrition against other growth or diminishment flows across a flexible time period in an easy to read waterfall chart moving from start period to end period.
  • To analyze what percentage of term products at maturity attrite, visit the Historic Roll Rates module.
  • To review the detailed definitions of each of balance flows, including granular sub-categories, visit the Methodology and Definitions sub-tab on the Balance Flows module.
  • There is also a short demo exploring attrition, refer to our Investigating Attrition 60 second demo.

 

How do I explore the full deposit relationship of customers opening new accounts?

 

Use the Vintage Customer Drill-down module in the Vintage Data section to analyze the full deposit relationship of customers, identifying groups of customers to analyze based on accounts they have opened or had active during a specific time period to determine other activity, like adding additional accounts.

1.    On the Vintage Customer Drill-down module from the Vintage Data section, use the Data Filters on the left side to narrow down the portfolio segmentation:
A.   Choose the Product of the campaign and any other identifying dimensions such as Segments targeted, Pricing Regions, etc. - for this example, let's look at CDs.
B.   Choose the Vintage Type, choosing between Standard Vintages, Legacy Vintages (if applicable), or vintage data sets affected by bank acquisitions, and then enter the Earliest Vintage and Latest Vintage dates to set the upper and lower data bounds.

This isolates the Vintages to be included in data output.

C.   To isolate the months of data to include in the analysis, enter the Start Date and End Date.

This is separate and distinct from the Earliest Vintage and Latest Vintage dates. For example, you could view the balances left in Vintages from January 2016 through June 2016, starting in July 2016 - with July 2016 selected in the Start Date field.

2.    Above the data chart, set the Chart Format drop-down options:
A.   To focus on acquisition dollars, change the Metric to Accounts.
B.   Set the Group By drop-down - let's try Product Name.

3.    Click the Update View button.

What to look for:

  • Assess whether you acquire accounts or attrite accounts. In this example, we start to lose accounts in March 2017 - specifically CDs. This is likely happening due to the term of the CD expiring. There does not seem to be any growth in accounts. If you wanted to ensure that the money wasn't leaving the bank, you would change the Metric to Balances and look at the $ amount.

Further analysis suggestions:

  • Explore attrited customers by changing the Customers selected to Attrited Customers Only.

 

How do I run a set of monthly reports that I define?

 

From the CDA section, there are two CDA Monthly Summary reports that provide a one-stop dashboard that display each of the other CDA Dashboards as a monthly overview (compiled on the last business day of each month). You can also use the Reporting section to define and create Report Packages that you can schedule to be run at a specific time each day, week, or month.
1.    From the Custom Analytics module of the CDA section, click the CDA Monthly Summary Dashboard.

The report is displayed with (currently) 13 pages you can tab through.

2.    To download the full multi-page report, click Download and choose Download PDF.
3.    From the dialog box, click the Include drop-down and choose This Story (this downloads all of the pages) and for best printing results, change the Orientation selection to Landscape. Then, click Create PDF.
4.    In addition, you can build a package of your own reports from the Reports module:

For detailed instructions, refer to the Using the Report Package Builder tab subsection.
