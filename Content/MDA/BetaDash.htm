Beta Dashboard

The Beta Dashboard allows you to view Betas for on-sale competitor products across all bank geographies, products, and reference rates over a selected date range. Data is available at the reference rate level for all in scope stock and custom reference rates.

Note: Data goes back to 2015 to capture several months in advance of the latest US rising rate environment. The primary data source for the dashboard is your institution’s 3rd party rate vendor (e.g., Informa) which drives the reference rate calculations across Market Analytics and the application as a whole.

Note: 2015 Data is only populated in the dashboard for certain "strategic tiers." Therefore, there are 5 balance tier buckets that represent a common span of tiering levels.

Calculations

Beta: The percent of each macro change passed through to the product rate

  • For example, between January 2017 and November 2018, the target Fed Funds rate increased 1.50% (from 0.75% to 2.25%). If during the same time period, the WAMP of a giving pricing cell increased by 1.00%, the beta for that cell would be 1.00%/1.50%, and displayed as 67%.

  • When aggregating cells in the Beta Dashboard, all rates displayed represent non-weighted averages across the selected dimensions.

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

This dashboard is intended to answer strategic questions like:

  • Are large banks or small banks responding with a higher beta for MMA products in the last x rate moves? Does it differ by product type?

  • For which products do direct banks show the highest betas?

  • Is the differentiation across my geographies in competitor betas, by product, or by competitor reference rate set?

 

How do I use this Module?

 

Use the Beta Dashboard to displays the Beta for different products at your institution over a selected date range, and compares it to competitor groups and reference rates of your choice (e.g., Large Banks, Small Banks, Weighted Max).

1.    Access the Market Data section and choose Custom Analytics).
2.    From the Novantas Workbooks table, click Beta Dashboard.
3.    From the left side of the module, use the Slider to narrow down the historical data set - data is available as early as January 2015 available.
4.    Optional: Still on the left side of the module, use the multi-select Data Filters on the left side to narrow down your institution's unique portfolio segmentation (i.e., Product Group, Term Bucket, etc.).

Here single or multi-select filters are available for the standard dimensions used across PriceTek's Market Data Analytics section for Channel, Line of Business, Pricing Market, Product Group, Term Bucket, and Tier Bucket.

Tip: To change the selection, click to check/un-check boxes and the click Apply.

5.    Optional: From above the module tables, use the Reference Rates drop-down to choose which rates to display in the column(s) in the top two tables as well as the lines in the Average Rate Over Time Chart.

Tip: To you change the selection, click to check/un-check boxes and the click Apply.

Remove reference rates for a less cluttered view in the three tables. For a full set of your Reference Rates and definitions, refer to the Reference Rates page.

6.    Still from above the module tables, use the Group By drop-down to choose either: Product Group, Term Bucket, Pricing Market, Tier Bucket, or Line of Business.

Your selection is reflected in how beta is grouped in the middle table. For example, choose Product Group and the middle table will display the Beta Across Product Group.

Tip: To you change the selection, click to check/un-check boxes and the click Apply.

  FAQs  
  What is the data source? Data is sourced from the 3rd party vendor (i.e., Informa) that populates the reference rates throughout PriceTek, and therefore reflects 'on sale' products only.
  Where can I see 'back book' betas? For US institutions subscribing to Comparative Deposit Analytics (CDA), a dashboard in that module will analyzes betas at the peer group level for both acquisition as well as whole portfolio products, encompassing off-sale products as well.
