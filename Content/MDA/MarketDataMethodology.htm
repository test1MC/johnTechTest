Introduction to Market Data Modules

 

Market Data Analytics provides a robust set of modules designed to analyze and monitor crucial aspects of the competitive landscape
Note: If your institution's PriceTek installation is not configured with various Pricing Markets (i.e., Direct Banks) then only the Ref Rates (Market) module will be active in your installation of PriceTek, as the other Market Data modules focus on geographic comparisons.

Market Data Analytics Module Module Description
Overview (Market)

In depth drill down into single Pricing Markets, showing competitor and client shares, as well as growth trends and various rate and elasticity metrics.

Note: This module will be transitioned to the central platform mid 2019. eHelp to this module will be added at that time.
Ref Rates (Footprint)

Dynamic report to compare absolute rate, reference rates, client rate and relative rates across all markets in the footprint for any given product group, and shows high/low and current position across any specified timeline
Ref Rates (Market) Single market rate summary showing reference rate data for any selected product group over time, as well as individual competitor rates, client rates, macro rates (ie. Fed Funds,) etc..

 

Understanding Reference Rate Settings

 

Reference Rate Introduction

 

Reference Rates are a key component of PriceTek as they provide a metric off of which we measure the bank’s relative price position.

   

To calculate Reference Rates, we get competitive data from two sources with specific calculation settings determined by your institution. The example below highlights the sources from a typical US based institution - note for international clients other sources are used for deposit share.
Understanding Reference Rates

Reference Rates are configured and created specific to each PriceTek institution, and will reflect your own competitors and calibration preferences. Click here to see the reference rates configured in your PriceTek implementation.

Understanding Large Bank, Small Bank, Internet Bank classifications

Typically, we classify competitor institutions into one of three categories and calculate both category-specific and aggregate reference rates.
Illustrative Large Bank Reference Rate

In general, Large Bank reference rates are weighted calculations. These are done by Deposit Share. Note your reference rates may be calibrated differently, Click here to see the reference rates configured in your PriceTek implementation.
Illustrative Small Bank Reference Rate

In general, a Small Bank Rate is calculated in order to incorporate small banks into the WAMP and Weighted Max. Note your reference rates may be calibrated differently, Click here to see the reference rates configured in your PriceTek implementation.
Illustrative Internet Bank Reference Rate

In addition, we calculate an Internet Bank rate comprised of the major Direct Bank rates. Note your reference rates may be calibrated differently, Click here to see the reference rates configured in your PriceTek implementation.
WAMP and Weighted Max Reference Rates

The WAMP considers the Large, Small and Internet Bank Rates to provide a proxy for average rate in a given market, while the Weighted Max serves as a proxy for the most competitive rate in a given market.

Note: Weight of Internet bank may vary depending on your PriceTek configuration. Click here to see the reference rates configured in your PriceTek implementation.

Using Market Data Analytics to Answer Strategic Questions

Market Segmentation

  • Which markets and products appear to offer grow opportunities (“cheap” and elastic) versus harvest opportunities (“expensive” and inelastic)?

Competitive Landscape

  • Which banks are the major players (high market share) in which products? Who is growing fast?

Understanding the Overview (Market) Module

 

The Overview (Market) module provides a market-level landscape overview and summarizes the competitive environment.

Overview

  • Drill down from footprint view to market-level view to examine individual competitors.
  • Understand your institution’s market position and competitive environment by tracking other banks based on various characteristics, including market share, recent growth, or deposit-branch ratio.

Column Definitions

The column definitions for both Deposits or Branches columns are the same:

  • Total: Deposits are shown in thousands, this is the total calculated retail deposits for this institution in the selected Pricing Market. Branches reflect total retail branches.
  • Share:  This institution's metric in this Pricing Market divided by the total metric in the selected Pricing Market.
  • Rank: Numeric rank against competitors.
  • 3 Year Growth: This institution's metric growth over the last 3 years of data available.
  • Growth / Market Growth: Represents if this institution is growing faster or slower than the market average in this category. 100% = same speed growth as the market. 200% = 2x faster growth than market average, etc.

Ratio column definitions:

  • Deps Per Branch: Total deposits, in thousands, per branch, for the institution in this Pricing Market.
  • Deposit Share / Branch Share: A measure of network equity, this is the Deposit Share metric / Branch Share metric. A value > 100% means the institution has a greater share of Deposits than would be proportionate based on their share of branches.

Definitions for the Institution columns:

  • Share: Identifies the share of this institution's total balances that are represented by the institution's balances in the selected Pricing Market.  For example, an institution with $10 billion in Retail Deposits would show 10% here for a Pricing Market that alone contained $1 billion in Retail Deposits.
  • 3 Year Change: How much the above Share metric has changed over the last three years. A positive number indicates this market is becoming a larger percentage of the institution's overall deposit portfolio.

Example

  • For the selected growth market, Pacific Heights:
    • Mountain Peak makes up a relatively small portion of Demo Bank’s Footprint (2%).
    • Demo Bank (highlighted row in the table) has a fairly high market share (23%, ranked 3rd).

 

Understanding the Ref Rates Modules

 

Understanding the Ref Rates (Footprint) Module

The Reference Rates (Footprint) module provides the ability to view competitive rates by market.

Overview

  • View either reference rates, your rate, or relative rates (bank’s rate relative to reference rate).

  • Compare historical trends (high-low analysis) by market across your footprint.

  • Identify which markets are cheaper (grow) or expensive (harvest).

  • Monitor those markets you are pricing up versus those where you are taking spread.

Examples

  • South Plains is the only market that is priced reasonably competitively relative to the market’s Weighted Max.

  • Demo Bank has become less competitive in all pricing markets other than South Plains.

Understanding the Ref Rates (Market) Module

The Reference Rates (Market) module provides a reference rate history and trends to help evaluate past strategies and develop future goals.

Overview

  • View historical rates to anticipate the upcoming rate environment to help set long-term goals and develop shorter-term strategies.

  • Identify which banks in a specific market are offering the most competitive rates and the individual players’ contributions to the overall WAMP.

Example

  • Key competitors increased rates in first quarter 2018.
  • Competitors have since leveled out or lowered rates slightly, potentially creating an opportunity to gain market share.
