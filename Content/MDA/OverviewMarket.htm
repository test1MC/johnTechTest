Overview (Market)

The Overview (Market) module displays deposit and market share data for your institution versus your competitors at a granular level, using the proprietary Branchscape database.  Branchscape is built annually from FDIC branch level datasets to clean and isolate retail deposits.

Note: For details on the methodology, refer to the Market Data Methodology page.

The column definitions for both Deposits or Branches columns are the same:

  • Total: Deposits are shown in thousands, this is the total calculated retail deposits for this institution in the selected Pricing Market. Branches reflect total retail branches.
  • Share:  This institution's metric in this Pricing Market divided by the total metric in the selected Pricing Market.
  • Rank: Numeric rank against competitors.
  • 3 Year Growth: This institution's metric growth over the last 3 years of data available.
  • Growth / Market Growth: Represents if this institution is growing faster or slower than the market average in this category. 100% = same speed growth as the market. 200% = 2x faster growth than market average, etc.

Ratio column definitions:

  • Deps Per Branch: Total deposits, in thousands, per branch, for the institution in this Pricing Market.
  • Deposit Share / Branch Share: A measure of network equity, this is the Deposit Share metric / Branch Share metric. A value > 100% means the institution has a greater share of Deposits than would be proportionate based on their share of branches.

Definitions for the Institution columns:

  • Share: Identifies the share of this institution's total balances that are represented by the institution's balances in the selected Pricing Market.  For example, an institution with $10 billion in Retail Deposits would show 10% here for a Pricing Market that alone contained $1 billion in Retail Deposits.
  • 3 Year Change: How much the above Share metric has changed over the last three years. A positive number indicates this market is becoming a larger percentage of the institution's overall deposit portfolio.

 

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

  • Reports from the field come in of a competitor with an aggressive rate that must be matched in order to remain competitive. This module can help identify how prominent that competitor is within each Pricing Market that your institution operates, and help inform a response strategy.
  • To monitor the performance of branches within each market, and flag areas where deposit share is below expected branch share, for consideration in bank or product strategies to remedy.
