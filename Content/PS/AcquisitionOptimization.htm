Acquisition Optimization

The Acquisition Optimization module allows you to access the multi-case scenarios your institution has created using the Scenario Builder (single rule scenarios only) to achieve the optimal way to hit acquisition balance or rate targets.

Choose one of the multi-case Scenarios (single rule scenarios only) created by either you or one of your peers.

Click Update View to display the optimization table after you have chosen a scenario and entered either a Target Balance or Target Rate.

Segmentation Filters and drop-down options will change as you choose different scenarios; select the scope of the portfolio to analyze.

Choose Balance to set a Target Value of an acquisition Balance (e.g., $227,000,000) or choose Rate to set a Target Value of an interest rate (e.g., 150 for 1.5%).

Enter the Target Value as either an acquisition balance value or a Rate value.

The Min Rate column displays the minimum rate you are willing to price in the Scenario.

The Max Rate column displays the maximum rate you are willing to pay in the Scenario.

The Base Rate column displays the “Base” rate in this Scenario. For this example, your institution is priced everywhere at 1.50% and the selected Scenario indicates you are willing to price down 50bps (to 100) or up 50bps (to 200) – and those are the “cases” that the Scenario will test, as well as everything in the range – 100, 105, 110, 115….195, 200.

The Optimized Rate is the ideal rate to pay per Pricing Market to attain the desired Target Value.

For this example, the Base Case at 1.50% across Pricing Markets allows for a an expected acquisition of ~346mm. Since we set an acquisition target of less than that - 346mm as well, the "optimizer" looks across the markets and suggests we up in some places (e.g., Central Valley, North Rockside) – and price down in other places (e.g., River Station, Capeside). I still end up with the same goal of 346mm, but we do it for cheaper – the varying rate indicates my overall rate paid can be dropped down to 1.37% from 1.50%.

Alternatively, if I change the Target Type to Rate , and set a rate of 1.50%, then we can keep our expected acquisition at 346mm, but we can strategically vary pricing.

Note: This page is limited to optimizing across geographies at the moment, and only does acquisition. It does not pay attention to how long the money stays or where it goes after x months.

 

How do I use this Module?

 

The Acquisition Optimization module allows you to access the multi-case scenarios your institution has created using the Scenario Builder (single rule scenarios only) to achieve the optimal way to hit acquisition balance or rate targets.

1.    Access from the Pricing Strategy section (choose Acquisition Optimization), and use the multi-select Data Filters on the left side to narrow down your institution's unique portfolio segmentation (i.e., product, Term Bucket, etc.).

Note: You must click to apply changes.

2.    Enter the Target Type drop-down and choose Balance or Rate
3.    Enter the Target Value - either a Balance value or a Rate value.
4.    Click .

The Acquisition Optimization table is displayed.

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
 
Optimization Algorithm Multi-Case Scenario to be Optimized

 

The optimization algorithm starts with a multi-case scenario and selects the best case for each pricing market subject to a global balance or rate target.
 
Optimization Pricing Strategy

 

The marginal cost of changing rates is used to find the cheapest method to grow balances.
 
Optimization Output

 

The optimization output will highlight the optimal cell for each market among all of the cases in the scenario.
