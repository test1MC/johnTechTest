Curve Comparison

The Curve Comparison module allows you to test pricing strategies by region, market, product, tier buckets, and term buckets. Open a second chart to compare two comparable metrics (e.g., two pricing regions) in order to strategize pricing points.

Use the drop-down arrow to choose from the various models that may have been calibrated as a reference for your institution. Click to see the model sets for your institution.

The Primary model set is traditionally the one used for Scenario calculations. For questions or requests to add model sets to use as reference, reach out to your Client Services representative(s).

Select the balance flow for which to view elasticity models.  Options include:
Acquisition - this is the Branch-normalized acquisition and switch in (augmentation) balance acquired, per the selected Metric, acquired at the X-Axis Value. Value is currency.

Percent Roll - this is the % of starting balances that stay with the bank in any capacity, either by going to their designated product or by switching to another product/term.

Note: Percent Roll is the inverse of attrition.

Percent Switch - this is % of balances that stay with the bank, but switch to another product/term.

Applicable for term products only: This designates how many times a product has "rolled" or renewed. (For example, a CD that reaches maturity, renews, and reaches a second maturity - vs a CD maturing for the very first time).

Note: This requires a level of data that we do not have for all clients.

Generally applies to liquid "teaser" or promotional products, this designates the months since a product "matured" from its promotion duration and transferred to the default post-promotion state. Because switch or attrition tends to decrease the farther a product gets from a "maturity", we traditionally utilize this bucketed time period as a model dimension.

This is your chart legend.

The Fitted Model line indicates modeled relationship between the in-scope actual datapoints and relevant independent variable while the Area of Indifference line indicates portions of the Fitted Model that demonstrate insignificant slope relative to the other areas of the model, i.e., areas where a change in relative rate in either direction results in minimal change to the dependent variable. Hover over Current Curve info to display the modeled reference rate and RSQ of the model.

Click the drop-down to assign color data points by year or By last 3 months . The default is All same (all data points are blue).

Tip: The larger data points are for the most recent data, and are size scaled to reflect the relative weight they have on the overall model. To help the model "learn" a changing rate environment more quickly, more recent datapoints carry a heavier model weight than older data points.

The Advertising drop-down is displayed only for your products where the effects are configured into the acquisition models. To configure advertising effects, Novantas requires historical rate-based advertising data.

The Metric drop-down is only available for the Acquisition Balance Flow. Choose either Per Branch or Total.

The X-Axis Value drop-down is only available for the Acquisition Balance Flow. Choose either Absolute Rate or Relative Rate.

The Absolute Rate is the translation of the relative rate of a given point in the model translated to an absolute rate equivalent in today's rate environment. For example, a point modeled with a relative rate of 30% below WAMP would, if the WAMP were 1.00% today, show up with an absolute rate of 70BPS.

The Relative Rate is the ratio between the Reference Rate and your institution rate.

Graphs can take several different curve types:

  • Cell-Level Curves are calibrated at the pricing cell level, and used in Scenario calculations. You’re looking at a cell-level curve if you drill down into specific portfolio segmentation (e.g., Monkey Market accounts in the Mountain region in Tier Bucket $0-$9,999).
  • Combo Curves are calibrated on a roll-up of segmentation across all tiers (or all markets). These curves are meant for elasticity viewing only, and are not used in Scenario calculations.

Download options include:

  • PPT: Screenshot of chart, with headers, in PowerPoint
  • PDF: Screenshot of chart, with headers, as a PDF
  • Excel: In this module only, this option will allow for the export of multiple Elasticity curves. The Curve Exporter dialog box is displayed, prompting you to select which dimension to cycle through. A curve is produced for each value in the selected dimensions, and stacked into a single Excel file (refer to the Tips and Tricks section below for details).
  • Excel Raw: Exports only the active curve to an Excel file, including the underlying data. Note: This differs from other modules in that it is a formatted export, and not raw data.

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

  • Understanding the "fit" of key models that influence Scenarios, to influence decisions on Scenario Output usage, application of Adjustment Rules, etc
  • Quickly visualize the price-volume relationship between specific products that have been modeled
  • Export multiple elasticity curves to scan for variations in elasticity across portfolio dimensions
  • Provide supporting evidence to broader internal teams related to price change decisions, highlighting elastic vs inelastic cells that drove the decision making process or influenced Scenario output

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
 

 

Maintaining a Consistent Relative Rate Over Time

 

Due to the changing reference rate environment - and in order to maintain consistent relative rate over time - historical data points have a shifting absolute rate.

  • The External Reference Rate (WAMP) increased between Nov. 2nd and Nov. 23rd, increasing the Acquisition Reference Rate. To represent the September 2018 data point at a consistent 10% Relative Rate across both weeks, each of our historical data points shifts. Thus, the ‘present day’ equivalent Acquisition APY shown on the chart increases.
  • When viewing elasticity curves by Absolute Rate across time periods, the historical points will change in x-axis value, but the Current Rate point will not change.
  • When viewing elasticity curves by Relative Rate across time periods, the historical points will not change in x-axis value, but the Current Rate point will change.
  • Note the Cross-Product (Internal Reference Rate component) can change over time and result in Relative Rate positions that do not always match the same data in Market Data Analytics, which includes no Internal Reference Rate component.

Note: Full reference rate components: Acquisition Reference Rate = External Reference Rate + [b*Internal Reference Rate*(Internal Reference Rate – Ext Reference Rate)].

  FAQs  
  Why can't I switch to the Absolute Rate for my Percent Roll or Percent Switch Curves? The relative rate calculation is more complex for the existing flows, and not as cleanly translatable to a single rate
  What data sources my current rate? This is generally sourced from the weekly Current Rates table sent from your institution to Novantas.
  Why is Sync Axes unavailable? To utilize Sync Axes, two charts of the same balance flow must be populated. For example, acquisition measured as a dollar cannot be sync'd with percent roll measured as a percentage of starting balances.
  How is the Relative Rate determined for my pricing curve? Each pricing cell models against a single external reference rate, which is chosen based on which provides the strongest fit – highest R2 – within the reference rate consideration set. The reference rate consideration set can be limited by portfolio segment, and these settings are customized for each client.
