Optimization Builder

A built-in wizard helps to build Optimizations including multi-month rate rules, rate environment responses and adjustment rules.

 

  Optimization Builder Step Description
  1) Name Optimization Set a Optimization name, description, and duration
  2) Set My Strategy Set a bank's rate to test, and then set any teaser duration, as well as any advertising schedule or other non-rate factors
  3) Set Rate Environment Set a forecast for the macro rate environment, and then set your bank and competitors responses to these rate changes, as well as competitor changes not triggered by a rate environment change.
  4) Set Adjustment Rules

Set rules to override modeled projections based on expert judgment.
  5) Optimization Constraints set the upper and lower bounds on your test rate, and the segmentation ordering.
  6) Optimization Targets Choose an Optimization Objective and then set your Target.
  7) Review and Run Optimization Review the Optimization parameters before running.

 

Name the Optimization

2.    Type a Optimization Description.

Tip: This description will be searchable and displayed under the Optimization Name on the Optimization Manager page. 3.    Click the Duration drop-down and choose the number of months out you wish to draw out your Optimization.

An exciting feature of Optimizations is the ability to set different rates in different months (e.g., set a new baseline strategy in month 1 and then a macro rate move - such as Fed Funds - in month 4 coupled with a competitor response).

Key Terms: Beta and Lag

Lag: The gap in time between a macro change and a product rate change

  • For example, if the Fed Funds rate increases in December and the lag is set to 1, the response is effective in January
  • Lags can be set to 0, 1 or 2 months

Beta: The percent of each macro change passed through to the product rate

  • For example, if the Fed Funds rate goes up by 25 bp and the beta is set to 0.8, the rate would increase by 25 * 0.8 = 20 bp
  • Enter a 60% beta as “0.60”

Key Adjustment Rule Applications

  • Only one “standard” Adjustment Rule can apply to a single cell in the same month- the bottom rule in the inventory applies.
    • Switch Allocation and Change Caps rules are considered “non-standard” and can be applied along with a "standard" rule.
    • Between Static Assumption, Dynamic Assumption, and Apply Adjacent Model rules though- only one can apply in a single month for a single cell.
  • Change Caps rules can apply at the same time as Dynamic Assumption and Apply Adjacent Model rules, but cannot apply at the same time as Static Assumption rules.
    • If a Change Caps and Dynamic Assumption rule apply in the same month, cap references are based on the original curve inputs (e.g., if the curve is shifted up 20% but extrapolation is capped at 0% above the highest historical data point, the cap is not shifted by the +20% Dynamic Assumption rule.
    • If there is no Change Caps rule in the same month, the default extrapolation caps are then shifted by the Value in the Dynamic Assumption rule (e.g., if the curve is shifted up 20%, the caps are also be shifted up 20%).
  • Order of Application:
 
1.    Apply the lowest adjustment rule in the inventory in this cell for this month (with the exception of Change Caps and Switch Allocation rules).
2.    If the active rule is not static and there is a Change Caps rule: Apply it.

If the active rule is not static and there is no Change Caps rule: Apply the default extrapolation.

3.    No matter what rule applies: Apply the global caps (e.g., 0% to 100% for Percent Attrition).

Key Terms when assigning an anchored rate:

  • Static BPSBasis point (BPS) refers to a common unit of measure for interest rates and other percentages in finance. One basis point is equal to 1/100th of 1%, or 0.01%, or 0.0001. For example, to set the rate to 1.25%, set the Rate Type to Static BPS and enter 125 in the Value text box.
  • WAMP The Weighted Average Market Price is the weighted average of all tracked brick and mortar banks (This is a Novantas term). Contact your PriceTek Client Services representative for more information about the data sources and calculations used for WAMP.
  • Weighted MaxThe Weighted average of either the Maximum large bank rate, small bank rate, or direct bank rate. Contact your PriceTek Client Services representative for more information about the data sources and calculations used for WAMP.
  • FTPThe acronym for Funds Transfer Price. Revenue calculations are based on the spread between rate and FTP.
  • Indexed to Prior Month's RateThis simplifies a potential workflow, allowing you to create multi-month rate effects anchored to your bank's prior month’s rate. For example:
    * To "step down" off sale rates by 10 bps once in month 1 and once in month 3, create two rules anchored at 10 bp below the Prior Month’s Rate.
    * To run a 3-month promotion, create a month-1 rule to increase rates by 50 bp from the Prior Month’s Rate, then create a month-4 rule to reduce rates by 50 bp from Prior Month’s Rate

Set Optimization Targets

First, choose an Optimization Objective and then set your Target.

Note: Control parameters have not yet been introduced into the Optimization algorithms. Therefore, to ensure your Optimization makes sense when you run it and it produces actionable metrics, make sure your Target is inline with your Objective. For example, do not set an Objective of minimizing interest expense a Target of a specific interest expense.

Choosing an Objective and Setting a Target
1.    Click the Objective drop-down and choose Maximize Average Balance, Maximize End Balance, Minimize End Weighted Rate, Minimize Total Interest Expense, Maximize Total Revenue, or Maximize Month 1 Acquisition.
2.    Click or .

What each Objective identifies:

  • Maximize Average BalanceMaximize the average End Balance across all months.
  • Maximize End Balance Maximize the End Balance for the final month of the duration.
  • Minimize End Weighted RateMinimize the weighted rate in the final month of the duration.
  • Minimize Total Interest ExpenseMinimize the sum of the Monthly Interest Expense (Rate / 12 * Balance) across all months.
  • Maximize Total RevenueMaximize the sum of the Monthly Spread Revenue ([FTP - Rate]+ / 12 * Balance) across all months).
    FTP is the acronym for Funds Transfer Price.
  • Maximize Month 1 AcquisitionMaximize acquisition balances in the first month.

6.    Enter the target Value.

What each Target establishes:

  • End BalanceThe target is a particular End Balance value for the final month of the duration - per the defined scope that meets the Objective. Note: Your Objective cannot also be End Balance-related.
  • End Balance Change The difference in End Balance value for the final month of the duration between Optimal and Baseline cases (per the defined scope) that meets the Objective. A Baseline case is a projection at current rates taking all other rules into account. Note: Your Objective cannot also be End Balance-related.
  • Average Balance The target is to achieve an average of End Balances across all months of the duration at the value set (per the defined scope) that also meets the Objective. Note: Your Objective cannot also be End Balance-related.
  • Average Balance Change difference in average of End Balances across all months of the duration between Optimal and Baseline cases (per the defined scope) that meets the Objective. A Baseline case is a projection at current rates taking all other rules into account. Note: Your Objective cannot also be Balance-related.
  • End Weighted Rate The target is to achieve an End Weighted Rate for the final month of the duration at the value set (per the defined scope) that meets the Objective. Note: Your Objective cannot also be Rate-related. To target an End Weighted Rate of 1.23%, enter 1.23 in the Value box.
  • End Weighted Rate Change difference in the Weighted Rate for the final month of the duration between Optimal and Baseline cases (per the defined scope) that meets the Objective. A Baseline case is a projection at current rates taking all other rules into account. Note: Your Objective cannot also be Rate-related. To target an End Weighted Rate increase of 15bp, enter 0.15 in the Value box.
  • Total Interest Expense The target is the sum of Monthly Interest Expense (Rate / 12 * Balance) across all months of the duration at the value set (per the defined scope) that meets the Objective. Note: Your Objective cannot be Rate-related.
  • Total Revenue The target is the sum of the Monthly Spread Revenue ([FTP - Rate] / 12 * Balance) across all months of the duration at the value set (per the defined scope) that meets the Objective. FTP is the acronym for Funds Transfer Price. Note: Your Objective cannot be Balance-related.
