Customer Drill-down

The Customer Drill-down module isolates specific groups of customers based on account and vintage attributes, and perform full portfolio analysis on those people over time.  

On this module the Data Filters are used to isolate Vintage and product attributes - and the dataset becomes, "all accounts of any customer that had an account meeting the criteria set in the Data Filters." 

  • For example, this module could be used to answer questions like "For the customers that participated in the January 2018 MMA Campaign in the Gold Product, what other products with the bank did they have at the time, or grow over time?"
  • The module can be thought of as a two step process. 1) Select products that define a set of customers. 2) Analyze *all accounts* of those customers.

Enter the Earliest Vintage date and the Latest Vintage date to isolate the Vintages to be included in data output.

Choose the Start Date and End Date to isolate the months of data included in the analysis.  This is separate and distinct from the Earliest/Latest Vintage data filters. For example, you could view the balances left in Vintages from January 2016 through June 2016 (set with the Earliest and Latest Vintage selections), starting in July 2016 - with July 2016 selected in the Start Date field.

Determines how accounts are being filtered. Choose Vintage to filter on Vintage, the date accounts originated. Alternatively, choose Existed On Date to capture accounts that were open with a positive balance on a given date, regardless of when they originated.

Note: When filtering by Vintage, it is possible to select a range of Vintages (using fields Earliest Vintage and Latest Vintage that will be visible). When filtering by Existed On Date, then those date fields are replaced by a single Existed on Date drop-down, and analysis can be performed based on a single month snapshot of existing accounts.

Click the drop-down to choose between Standard Vintages, Legacy vintages (if applicable), or vintage data sets affected by bank acquisitions. This drop-down is institution-specific. For more details, refer to the Understanding Account Origin subheader on the Vintage Data Methodology page.

Choose one of the following:

Balances: The total balance amount that stays with the bank.

Accounts: The total number of accounts that stay with the bank.

Weighted Rate: Measures the annual rate of the accounts as of the period end date and is not calculated based on interest dollars paid.

Average Bal/Acct: The average total balance amount in each account.

Customers: The number of customers that stay with the bank.

Choose either Time, a portfolio dimension or calculated Vintage metric to display on the X-Axis.  

Note: If choosing a non-time value, be sure to actively note the Metric Aggregation used to combine data from multiple time periods.

Choose one of your institutional Data Filters (e.g., Line of Business, Product Group), or choose CD Renewal Status (i.e., First Maturity or Multiple Maturities), Account Origin, Vintage Month, Vintage Quarter, or Vintage Year. You can also choose None.

Choose either:

  • Sum: The total amount over a selected time period (only accessible if the X-Axis Value is Time ); calculated by dividing the total remaining metric by the total metric’s starting value, independent of Vintage.
  • Average Over Observation: The average for the selected Metric across the selected X-Axis Value. Not accessible if the X-Axis Value is Time.
  • Monthly Average: The monthly average for the selected Metric across the selected X-Axis Value. Not accessible if the X-Axis Value is Time.
  • Weighted Average: The weighted annual rate of accounts as of the period end date across the selected X-Axis Value. Only pertains if the Metric selected is Weighted Rate.

Account Origin reflects the manner in which an account was originated. Each account gets this label only once, when it first appears. There are Standard and Granular views of this.

  • Standard Origin (default): This encompasses accounts originating with one of the identifiable PriceTek Balance Flows (Acquisition or Switch In).
  • Choose the drop-down and either click the Granular radio button and toggle on or off Granular Origins such as:
    • Customer Acquisition, Account Acquisition, Switch In: These make up the "Standard Origin" category and reflect accounts originating in these ways.
    • Legacy: Legacy captures the accounts that were present in the first month of data loaded into PriceTek, where we cannot view one period prior to associate any with being new, how, etc. Legacy captures these balances that existed at the start of the dataset.
  • Depending on your institution's configuration, there may be other Account Origins present to analyze (for example, accounts originated in a specific time period through a peer bank acquisition).

Click Update View to display the chart and table after you have made your Chart Format selections (e.g., Metric, Group By).

Click the Hide Model Exclusions checkbox to prevent any acknowledged outliers from being displayed in the data (set up per institution).

The Group Insignificant Items checkbox aggregates insignificant items in the chart and table to a single Other bucket, to keep the output cleaner and easier to read in situations with multiple but insignificant data elements in the Group By selection.

Hover over a line or column to display the metrics displayed in the table view.

Click the Table Tab to display the hover-over metrics from the chart in a table.

Choose either:

  • All (default): All customers include both retained and attrited.
  • Retained Customers Only: Those customers that retained at least one deposit account across the selected date range.
  • Attrited Customers Only: Those customers that closed all accounts by the end of the selected date range.

The legend is interactive and any bar item can be clicked to remove or add back to the visualization.

 

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

  • Monitor whether or not promotional campaigns are effectively bringing in customers that grow their relationship with the bank.
  • Track the effectiveness of product fencing.
  • Develop a better understanding of the holistic customer relationship.
  • Generate a customer level dataset that can be leveraged for new custom reports.

 

How do I use this Module?

 

The Vintage Customer Drill-down module displays data for all accounts a customer has with the bank, based on filter selections that identify a customer pool to analyze.

For example, selecting "Vintage 201701, Product Group Money Market" in the filters will set the analysis to be any customer that opened a Money Market account in 201701. Dimension selections narrow down to accounts of interest, and the module displays customer data for customers associated with those accounts.

1.    Access the Vintage Data section (choose Customer Drilldown), and use the multi-select Data Filters on the left side to narrow down your institution's unique portfolio segmentation (i.e., Product Group, Term Bucket, etc.).

The default starting selections are All for all Segmentation Filters. This will define what accounts determine the customer pool to analyze.

2.    Choose whether to isolate accounts based on when they opened, or when they existed at a point in time: Use the Filter By drop-down, choose Existed On Date to isolate customers based on accounts they had at a given time period.

Choose Vintages to isolate customers based on accounts they opened in a given time period range. If you choose Existed On Date, then the Earliest Vintage and Latest Vintage date fields are replaced by a single Existed on Date drop-down.

3.    Click the Vintage Type drop-down and choose between Standard Vintages, Legacy vintages (if applicable), or vintage data sets affected by bank acquisitions.

This drop-down is institution-specific. For more details, refer to the Understanding Account Origin subheader on the Vintage Data Methodology page.

4.    Enter the Earliest Vintage and Latest Vintage dates to set the upper and lower data bounds (not applicable for Existed On Date views).

This isolates the Vintages to be included in data output.

5.    Enter the Start Date and End Date you want to isolate the months of data included in the analysis.

These fields are not applicable to both Percent Balances Remaining and Percent Accounts Remaining metrics.

Note: You must click to apply changes.

6.    Select a metric and relevant choices to display, aggregate, and/or group the output using the applicable Chart Format drop-downs - Metric, X-Axis Value, Group By, Metric Aggregation, Customers, and Account Origin.

This organizes your data for different views - and each view tells a different story.

Nuances to this module

  • Use the Hide Model Exclusions and Group Insignificant Items checkboxes to remove outliers and group items for better clarity. Hover over these drop-down in the above screenshot for full details. Note: If the box(es) are grayed out, they are not relevant to the Chart Format items you selected.
  • From the Legend on the right side of the page, toggle on and off any of the metrics displayed. Click All to display all or None to turn off all of the metrics (and then turn back on one at a time).
  • Toggle between the Value and Percent radio buttons.
  • Click the Table tab to view the underlying data.

 

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
  Refer to the Vintage Data Methodology for details.
  Data Dictionary
  Vintage The date an account first appears in the data history, assigned monthly.
  Account Origin The manner in which an account has originated. Each account gets this label only once, when it first appears.
  Vintage Exclusions Specific accounts may be excluded from the analysis due to non-customer activity, outlier behavior or other data anomalies. Vintage exclusions are unique to the Vintages module, and can be hidden with the visible checkboxes.
  Vintage Range Represented in PriceTek with Earliest / Latest Vintage. This isolates the Vintages to be included in data output. Typically, Legacy and Bank Acquisition origins will only map to a single Vintage
  Date Range Represented in PriceTek with Start Date / End Date. This isolates the months of data is included in the analysis. This differs from the Vintage Range. For example, one could analyze “accounts that originated in January 2013” (January ‘13 Vintage Range) across the full year 2013 (Date Range).
  Months Out Represents the number of months since account origination.

 

Tips, Tricks, & FAQs
  Tips & Tricks  
  Click the Table Tab to display the hover-over metrics from the chart in a table format.
  FAQs  
  Why do I see months prior to my vintage month w/data in Customer Drilldown but not in Vintage Data Summary? Customer Drilldown displays the full customer deposit portfolio based on a specific vintage selection. Customers in your vintage selection could potentially have had accounts and balances prior to the month if Account Acquisition or Switch In is selected in Account Origin. Vintage Data Summary only displays the balances and accounts associated with your vintage selection, therefore there should not be data prior to your Start Vintage Date.
