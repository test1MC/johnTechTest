Vintage Data Summary

The Vintage Data Summary module displays decay curves for any portfolio segment to determine what percent of balances or accounts remain months or years after an account opening. You can:

  • Track retention performance of acquired portfolios or specific balance “vintages.”
  • View portfolio balance analytics with “vintage” as an identifier.

Enter the Earliest Vintage date and the Latest Vintage date to isolate the Vintages to be included in data output.

Choose the Start Date and End Date to isolate the months of data included in the analysis.  This is separate and distinct from the Earliest/Latest Vintage data filters. For example, you could view the balances left in Vintages from January 2016 through June 2016 (set with the Earliest and Latest Vintage selections), starting in July 2016 - with July 2016 selected in the Start Date field.

Note: To ensure rational data, Start and End Date selections are not available viewing metrics for both Percent Balances Remaining and Percent Accounts Remaining metrics.

Determines how accounts are being filtered. Choose Vintage to filter on Vintage, the date accounts originated. Alternatively choose Existed On Date to capture accounts that were open with a positive balance on a given date, regardless of when they originated.

Note: When filtering by Vintage, it is possible to select a range of Vintages (using fields Earliest Vintage and Latest Vintage that will be visible). When filtering by Existed On Date, then those date fields are replaced by a single Existed on Date drop-down, and analysis can be performed based on a single month snapshot of existing accounts. When using Existed On Date, starting balances used in % Remaining calculations are based on each account's balance as of the Existed On Date, and not the account's true origination period balance.

Click the drop-down to choose between Standard Vintages, Legacy vintages (if applicable), or vintage data sets affected by bank acquisitions. This drop-down is institution-specific. For more details, refer to the Understanding Account Origin on the Vintage Data Methodology page.

Choose one of the following:

  • Percent Balances Remaining: The percent of balances that stay with the bank over the selected period of time. Balances included are from accounts matching the dimension filters at their origin, or vintage year/month. Metric is effectively: (Balances remaining in a given month) / (Balances in the month of origin).
  • Percent Accounts Remaining: The percent of accounts that stay with the bank over the selected period of time. Accounts included are from accounts matching the dimension filters at their origin, or vintage year/month. Metric is effectively: (Accounts remaining in a given month) / (Accounts in the month of origin).
  • Balances: The sum of balances that exist in a given time period that are from the selected starting vintage.
  • Accounts: The sum of accounts that exist in a given time period that are from the selected starting vintage.
  • Weighted Rate: Measures the annual rate of the accounts as of the period end date and is not calculated based on interest dollars paid.
  • Count of Vintages: The number of individual vintages present in any given data point. This is useful in identifying the amount of history that makes
  • Average Bal/Acct: The average total balance amount in each account.

Choose either:

  • Months Out: Data is normalized to show the number of months since account origination. For example, the "2" data point would reflect the March 2018 values for a January 2018 Vintage (2 months since origination), averaged with the April 2018 values for a February 2018 Vintage, etc.
  • Date: Data is shown in the actual date where the value is calculated.

Choose one of your institutional Data Filters (e.g., Line of Business, Product Group), or group by Vintage specific attributes that are calculated, such as:

  • Account Origin: The way in which the accounts in the Vintage were created (i.e., Customer Acquisition, Account Attrition, Switch In, etc.).
  • Vintage Month, Vintage Quarter, or Vintage Year: Averages are created across Vintages in these respective time buckets
  • You can also choose None.

Choose either:

  • Fully Weighted: The percent remaining is calculated by dividing the total remaining metric by the total metric’s starting value, independent of Vintage.
  • Average of Vintages: The straight average percent remaining of each Vintage in the dataset. Each Vintage is calculated independently, and those values are then averaged.

Account Origin reflects the manner in which an account was originated. Each account gets this label only once, when it first appears. There are Standard and Granular views of this.

  • Standard Origin (default): This encompasses accounts originating with one of the identifiable PriceTek Balance Flows (Acquisition or Switch In).
  • Choose the drop-down and either click the Granular radio button and toggle on or off Granular Origins such as:
    • Customer Acquisition, Account Acquisition, Switch In: These make up the "Standard Origin" category and reflect accounts originating in these ways.
    • Legacy: Legacy captures the accounts that were present in the first month of data loaded into PriceTek, where we cannot view one period prior to associate any with being new, how, etc. Legacy captures these balances that existed at the start of the dataset.
  • Depending on your institution's configuration, there may be other Account Origins present to analyze (for example, accounts originated in a specific time period through a peer bank acquisition).

This column is only displayed when the X Axis selection is Months Out. It will default to a number that ensures all data on the screen is made up of an average of a reasonable number of Vintages.

For example, if your institution has 60 months of data loaded in PriceTek, we have 59 "Vintages" that have been with the bank long enough to have decay data for 2 months out, 58 that have passed 3 months out, etc. There is only one Vintage, however, to show average decay 60 months out. Since this data point would be made of the average of only a single Vintage, it is unreliable - like the datapoint of 59 months out would be with only 2 Vintages being averaged. For this reason, the chart will by default only show X months out such that X represents a substantial number of Vintages being averaged into each dataset.

The legend is interactive and any line item can be clicked to remove or add back to the visualization.

 

Common Use Cases for this Module

 

  • Identify the portfolio segments with the most favorable retention behavior to support marketing or product decisions.
  • Measure the success of balances associated with specific campaigns or promotions.

 

How do I use this Module?

 

The Vintage Data Summary module displays decay curves for any portfolio segment to determine what percent of balances or accounts remain months or years after an account opening.

1.    Access the Vintage Data section (choose Vintage Data Summary ), and use the multi-select Data Filters on the left side to narrow down your institution's unique portfolio segmentation (i.e., Product Group, Term Bucket, etc.).

The default starting selections are All for all Segmentation Filters.

2.    Select the bucket of accounts to analyze, either based on their 'Vintage' (origination period) or based on accounts that existed at a specified point in time: click the Filter By drop-down, choose either Existed On Date or Vintages.

If you choose Existed On Date, then the Earliest Vintage and Latest Vintage date fields are replaced by a single Existed on Date drop-down.

3.    Click the Vintage Type drop-down and choose between Standard Vintages, Legacy vintages (if applicable), or vintage data sets affected by bank acquisitions.

This drop-down is institution-specific. For more details, refer to the Understanding Account Origin subheader on the Vintage Data Methodology page.

4.    Enter the Earliest Vintage and Latest Vintage dates to set the upper and lower data bounds (not applicable for Existed On Date views).

This isolates the Vintages to be included in data output.

5.    Enter the Start Date and End Date you want to isolate the months of data included in the analysis.

These fields are not applicable to both Percent Balances Remaining and Percent Accounts Remaining metrics.

Note: You must click to apply changes.

6.    Select a metric and relevant choices to display, aggregate, and/or group the output using the applicable Chart Format drop-downs - Metric, X-Axis Value, Group By, Metric Aggregation, Max Months Out, and Account Origin.

This organizes your data for different views - and each view tells a different story.

Nuances to this module

  • Use the Hide Model Exclusions and Group Insignificant Items checkboxes to remove outliers and group items for better clarity. Hover over these drop-down in the above screenshot for full details. Note: If the box(es) are grayed out, they are not relevant to the Chart Format items you selected.
  • From the Legend on the right side of the page, toggle on and off any of the metrics displayed. Click All to display all or None to turn off all of the metrics (and then turn back on one at a time).
  • Click the Table tab to view the underlying data.

 

Methodology and Definitions

  Methodology  
  Refer to the Vintage Data Methodology for details.
  Data Dictionary
  Vintage The date an account first appears in the data history, assigned monthly.
  Account Origin The manner in which an account has originated. Each account gets this label only once, when it first appears.
  Vintage Exclusions Specific accounts may be excluded from the analysis due to non-customer activity, outlier behavior or other data anomalies. Vintage exclusions are unique to the Vintages module, and can be hidden with the visible checkboxes.
  Vintage Range Represented in PriceTek with Earliest / Latest Vintage. This isolates the Vintages to be included in data output. Typically, Legacy and Bank Acquisition origins will only map to a single Vintage.
  Date Range Represented in PriceTek with Start Date / End Date. This isolates the months of data is included in the analysis. This differs from the Vintage Range. For example, one could analyze “accounts that originated in January 2013” (January ‘13 Vintage Range) across the full year 2013 (Date Range).
  Months Out Represents the number of months since account origination.
